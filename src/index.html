Why not just use…

Spotlight?

Great for opening apps. Not great when the thing you need is a link you saved three weeks ago, buried in a browser you've already closed.

Browser bookmarks?

You'd have to remember which browser, which folder, and what you named it. Kobi searches by content, not by what you remembered to label.

A note app?

Notes are great for writing. But your saved links, PDFs, and project files live elsewhere. Kobi connects the dots across all of them.

🔒

Your data never leaves your Mac.

No accounts. No cloud sync. No telemetry on your content. Kobi indexes everything locally and the code is open source — inspect every line on GitHub.
